**When to run.** _The trigger — concrete and observable. A specific signal in the brief, a customer email, a date passing, a number crossing a line._

**Goal.** _What "done" looks like in one sentence._

**Pre-checks.** _What to verify before starting (data freshness, who else is on this, sensitive timing)._

**Steps.**
1. _First action — who does it, what they say/send/check._
2. _Second action — including the decision point: if X, branch to step Y._
3. _Third action — keep going until the goal._

**Decision points.** _Named branches when the situation diverges from the happy path. "If the customer pushes back, escalate to Jeff." "If the AR ages another 15 days, switch to dispute mode."_

**Escalation.** _Who to pull in and when._

**Capture.** _What to record back into the wiki when the playbook completes — outcome, lessons, anything that should update the playbook itself or a related register (risk, decision journal)._

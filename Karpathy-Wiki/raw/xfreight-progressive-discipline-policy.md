Purpose

Establishes a consistent, documented framework for addressing driver performance
and safety violations across X-Trux, Inc. (DOT #841776 / MC #375851) and Truk-Way
Leasing employee drivers. Protects XFreight's CSA BASIC scores, Great West Casualty
insurance standing (via Acrisure), and shipper relationships while treating drivers
fairly and uniformly.

This policy codifies XFreight's existing informal practice (written warnings drafted
by Jeff, filed by Audra in Sharefile) into a formal, citable framework. It should be
incorporated into the next revision of the X-Trux Driver Safety Manual (last revised
January 1, 2022, maintained by Audra Newman).


Section 1 — Owner-Operators (X-Trux Contract Carriers)

5-Level Progressive Discipline

LevelTriggerActionLevel 1 — Verbal CounselingFirst minor incident: incomplete DVIR, minor speeding, low-severity safety eventVerbal counseling + mandatory retraining or coaching session; documented in driver fileLevel 2 — Written WarningRepeat minor incident or failure to correct Level 1 issueWritten warning letter + enrollment in safety improvement program; filed in Sharefile incident archiveLevel 3 — Strong Written NoticeModerate incident, repeat of Level 2, or first serious violationStronger written notice + extended coaching + load restrictions (no high-value, time-critical, or specialized loads)Level 4 — Load SuspensionSerious or repeated violation; failure to improve after Level 3Suspension from preferred/primary loads for 7–30 days (length at management discretion based on severity)Level 5 — Contract ActionSevere or repeated violation; failure to improve after Level 4Contract suspension or termination

Entry Points for Severe Incidents

The following bypass Levels 1–2 and enter at Level 3 or higher:


Preventable accidents
Major moving violations (reckless driving, excessive speed, failure to obey traffic control)
Scale violations (overweight citations)
Moving citations that affect CSA BASIC scores


Management judgment determines Level 3 vs. Level 4 entry based on severity and history.

Immediate Contract Termination (No Progressive Steps)


Fatal accident where driver is found at fault
DUI or controlled substance violation (federal DOT drug/alcohol rules apply)
Reckless driving causing injury to any person
Gross negligence: leaving the scene of an accident, falsifying logs/DVIRs or any
regulatory record, ignoring critical safety warnings resulting in major vehicle damage or injury
Any repeated Level 4 offense

Section 3 — Documentation Standards

All disciplinary actions must be documented:


Name the incident specifically — date, location, DOT inspection number if applicable
Cite the regulation — 49 CFR Part reference where a federal violation is involved
State expectations — what behavior change is required and by when
Driver acknowledgment — driver signs and dates the written warning (or refusal to sign is noted)
Filing — copy goes to driver's Sharefile incident folder (Audra files); Jeff and JB retain
working copies for serious matters


Drafting: Jeff Hannahs drafts warning letters; Audra Newman files and maintains the
incident record. Subject line pattern uses driver first name (e.g., "Brad") for quick
inbox recognition.

Drivers showing "improving — keep it up" or "falling fast — keep it up" trend phrases

Section 5 — Coaching Backlog Integration (Samsara Page 3)

Drivers with ≥2 unacknowledged safety events appear on the Coaching Needs Assigned list.


Unacknowledged coaching after 14 days = Level 1 trigger (verbal counseling on
failure to complete the coaching program)
Coaching ack is derived from Samsara SafetyEvents coachingStatus field
(coached / dismissed / recognized = acked)
Drivers stay on the coaching list until all events in the 30-day window are acked,
then roll off after 3 additional days



Section 6 — CSA / Insurance Implications

Management must weigh CSA BASIC impact when setting discipline level. Violations on
X-Trux's FMCSA MCMIS record (DOT #841776) stay for 24 months (inspections) or
60 months (crashes).

BASIC intervention thresholds:


Unsafe Driving / Crash Indicator: 65th percentile
All other BASICs (including Maintenance): 80th percentile


Accumulation toward intervention thresholds warrants accelerated discipline levels.
Great West Casualty (via Acrisure / Jami Hewitt) may independently require removal
of a driver from covered equipment — that determination supersedes this policy.

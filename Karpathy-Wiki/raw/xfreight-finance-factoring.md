## How factoring connects to the pipeline

Per Alvys's `Understanding Factoring in Alvys | Help Center` doc, factoring in Alvys involves:

1. **Setup** — wire the factoring company into Alvys
2. **Invoicing** — invoices flow normally
3. **Batch submission** — invoices are batched and sent to the factor
4. The Alvys → Power BI feed (and the daily scorecard's QB-vs-Alvys reconciliation) reflects whether invoices are factored or not — the AR aging buckets on page 1 of the brief and the customer-level reconciliation on page 12 are affected by factoring timing.

## Strategic notes

- **Why factoring matters now:** the AGCO RFP terms are 60-day payment. If XFreight wins material AGCO volume, the cash-flow gap during the 60-day window is significant. Factoring closes the gap (carrier gets paid in days, factor waits the 60).
- **Decision pending:** the comparison sheet was last touched 2025-10-02. The factoring vendor has not been finalized in the artifacts I can see.
- **Brokerage side (X-Linx) has different needs:** eCapital's offer references "Broker's Funds" and Carrier-side Quick Pay, suggesting they're targeting the X-Linx brokerage side. The asset side (X-Trux) factoring would be for X-Trux's customer invoices.

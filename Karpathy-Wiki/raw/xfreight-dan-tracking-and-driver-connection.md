# Dan — tracking style + driver connection (captured 2026-06-15)

> Completes the three-person tracking-style picture started in
> `xfreight-jeff-vs-jb-tracking-philosophy.md` (Jeff + JB). Also
> captures Dan's distinctive operational lens — driver-connectedness —
> which has product implications.

## Tracking style — facts-first, but skeptical

- **Closest to Jeff's mental model:** facts-first. Wants only the
  numbers that are already real (delivered, billed, invoiced), not
  forecasts.
- **One sharp difference from Jeff:** Dan is **very leery on the
  numbers** — he reads any reported figure with the intent of trying
  to **prove it wrong**. He doesn't trust a number until he's pushed on
  it and it survives.
- **Practical implication for any brief sent to Dan:** every metric
  needs to be **defensible at the source**. When Dan asks "where did
  this number come from," the answer must be traceable to a single
  cell / load / record — not a model output, not a smoothed average
  without provenance.
- **Product principle:** include the underlying counts and links to
  raw rows on Dan's pages. The MPG / speed / idle pages on today's
  Operational section are already shaped this way (every driver line
  has a verifiable underlying record); keep that pattern.

## Driver connection — distinctive operational lens

- Dan is the leadership-team member **most connected with the
  drivers**. He talks to them frequently, knows their personal
  situations, and tracks individual driver context that the rest of
  the team doesn't.
- He **wants as much information as possible** about each driver —
  operational, safety, financial, location, status.
- Implication: the planned **Operations brief** (per
  `xfreight-brief-roadmap.md`) should be the most data-dense of the
  briefs — Dan will read deeply, not skim. Don't optimize his pages
  for brevity at the cost of detail.

## Why this matters

**AGCO is a major prospect.** AGCO North America spends **$50M+ annually** on truckload freight. Even a partial award could be material to X-Trux + X-Linx revenue.

## Award structure

- AGCO will NOT award all business to one carrier
- Awarding by **best price per lane** + capacity
- 3 matrices: High-Volume TL lanes, Point-to-State pairs, State-to-Point pairs
- **High-Volume takes billing priority**, then State-to-Point, then Point-to-State
- AGCO will tell winning carriers if they're "primary on a lane" so capacity can be planned

## Equipment types accepted

- US 53ft Van
- US 53ft Van (Hazmat / Temp Control) — reefer in winter
- US Flatbed, Flatbed OB, Flatbed Oversized
- US 53ft Flatbed OB (Drop Trailer Preferred — else 4 free loading hours)
- US FBTSK/CT (Flatbed with Tarp / Side Kits / Conestoga)
- US RGN OB, RGN w/Slideouts
- US Stepdeck OB, 53ft Stepdeck OB (Drop Trailer Preferred — else 4 free loading hours)

## Payment + ops

- **Standard payment terms:** 60 days
- **AGCO iTMS** — AGCO's own TMS. Carriers must accept tenders, update status (pickup/delivery) through iTMS.
- **Fuel surcharge:** standard AGCO TL FSC table — only addition to base rate allowed
- **Accessorials:** standard AGCO accessorial table
- **No transloading** allowed (loads picked + delivered on same trailer). Transloading = lane removal + possible network removal.
- AGCO buys the entire truck — no co-loading with non-AGCO freight allowed.

## Strategic notes for XFreight

- X-Trux fleet (asset) handles the dedicated/contract lanes; X-Linx handles the brokerage capacity on spot/overflow.
- AGCO requires Hazmat/Temp Control equipment for some Van lanes — XFreight's current fleet is dry van, so this would limit XFreight to the non-Hazmat Van lanes unless reefer capacity is added (likely via X-Linx brokering to a reefer carrier).
- AGCO accepts multi-stop loads (Pick→Pick→Pick→Pick→Drop or Pick→Drop→Drop combinations) — fits XFreight's existing multi-stop capabilities (Billion Auto pattern is similar).
- 60-day payment terms — slow. Factoring options (Pathward, Triumph, OTR, eCapital) become relevant if AGCO award is large enough to strain cash flow. See `xfreight-finance-factoring.md`.

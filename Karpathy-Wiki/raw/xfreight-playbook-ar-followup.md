# Playbook — AR follow-up (30 / 60 / 90 day) (seed 2026-06-14)

> Source-of-record for the compiled `wiki/playbook-ar-followup.md`.
> Captures XFreight's collections process across AR aging buckets,
> tied to the brief's AR aging tile and the QB AR Aging Detail report.
> Edit by appending.

**When to run.** Continuously, but each bucket has a trigger:
- **31-60 days past due** — first formal nudge cycle.
- **61-90 days past due** — escalation cycle (Jeff involved).
- **91+ days past due** — pre-dispute / pre-factoring-recourse cycle (JB involved, factoring partner notified).
- Any single invoice > $10K crossing the 60-day mark, regardless of bucket totals.

**Goal.** Collect within the bucket without burning the relationship; for unsavable AR, get it to dispute resolution or write-off with documented effort.

**Pre-checks.**
1. Pull the QB AR Aging Detail report for X-Trux + X-Linx (JW excluded).
2. Cross-check with the brief's QB-vs-Alvys reconciliation: is the invoice missing or duplicated?
3. Confirm the invoice was actually delivered to the customer (factoring or direct send).
4. Check if there's an active dispute open on this customer (see Active Disputes page).

**Steps — 31-60 bucket.**
1. **Day 31** — Audra (AP/AR) sends a polite reminder email referencing the invoice number, amount, and original due date. Form letter, CC the dispatcher contact.
2. **Day 38** — if no response, phone call from Audra to the customer's AP contact.
3. **Day 45** — if still unpaid, escalate to the customer's dispatcher or account owner with a copy of the BOL/POD.
4. **Day 55** — if still unpaid, Audra hands to Jeff with a one-line context note. Move to 61-90 bucket triggers.

**Steps — 61-90 bucket.**
1. **Day 61** — Jeff (BD) calls the customer's escalation point (usually the VP/Controller, not AP). Frame as relationship preservation, not collections.
2. **Day 70** — written follow-up with payment plan offer if cash is the issue. Acceptable plans: 50% within 14 days + remainder within 30. Anything longer = escalate.
3. **Day 85** — JB notified. Pre-decision on whether this is a "collect aggressively" or "write-off planning" account.

**Steps — 91+ bucket.**
1. **Day 91** — Stop accepting new tenders from this customer pending resolution. Email confirmation to Dan (ops) so dispatch is aligned.
2. **Day 95** — JB + Jeff + Audra meeting: dispute, collections agency, factoring recourse (if applicable), or write-off?
3. **Day 100** — Decision actioned. If factoring recourse: notify the factoring partner per their contract terms. If collections: outside agency engaged with full documentation packet.
4. **Quarterly** — review write-off list; any recovered amount reopens the customer relationship discussion.

**Decision points.**
- **If the customer cites a service issue** — branch to customer-escalation playbook; AR follow-up pauses pending resolution.
- **If the AR is on a factored invoice with recourse** — the factoring partner's contract clock starts; we may owe the invoice back to factoring before the customer pays.
- **If multiple customers from the same broker show aging together** — investigate broker-side payment delay, not individual customer issues.
- **If a customer enters bankruptcy** — immediate switch to creditor-claim mode; outside counsel.

**Escalation.**
- Jeff at 60 days.
- JB at 85 days.
- Outside counsel at 100 days or for any disputed amount >$15K.
- Factoring partner notification per their recourse window (varies by partner — Pathward, Triumph, OTR, eCapital each have different terms).

**Capture.**
- Append outcome to this playbook's run log (customer, invoice, days, outcome, $ collected).
- If pattern: same customer recurring in 60+ bucket, add to risk register as "concentration risk for X" or "weakening AR pattern with X."
- If a process change is triggered (e.g., move customer to upfront pay, switch factoring partner), add to decision journal.

XFreight's formal framework for addressing driver performance and safety violations
across X-Trux owner-operators and Truk-Way employee drivers. Codifies the existing
informal practice (written warnings drafted by Jeff, filed by Audra in Sharefile)
into a citable 5-level structure with clear escalation triggers, documentation
standards, and immediate termination grounds.
Status: Drafted June 16, 2026. Pending JB Sweere sign-off. To be incorporated
into the next X-Trux Driver Safety Manual revision (Audra Newman, last issued
January 1, 2022).
Summary
Two parallel 5-level tracks — one for owner-operators (contract action at Level 5),
one for Truk-Way employees (termination at Level 5). Both feed directly from the
daily scorecard: Samsara page 4 speed flags map to discipline entry points; the
coaching backlog on page 3 triggers Level 1 if unacknowledged ≥14 days. CSA BASIC
accumulation accelerates the escalation path. Severe incidents skip Levels 1–2.
Certain acts trigger immediate termination with no progressive steps.
Key Ideas

Two tracks: Owner-operators (load suspension → contract termination) and
Truk-Way employees (unpaid suspension → employment termination). Levels 1–3
are structurally identical.
Samsara page 4 is the primary trigger feed. Speed flags map directly to
discipline levels; Bottom Line escalation two weeks running = automatic Level 2.
Documentation standard is non-negotiable. Every action names the incident,
cites the 49 CFR Part, states expectations, requires driver signature, and is
filed by Audra in Sharefile.
Great West Casualty can override. Acrisure/Jami Hewitt may require driver
removal from covered equipment independent of internal discipline level.
CSA impact accelerates escalation. Violations approaching BASIC intervention
thresholds warrant jumping levels.

Immediate Termination Grounds (Both Tracks)
No progressive steps — applies to owner-operators (contract) and employees (employment):

Fatal accident where driver is at fault
DUI or controlled substance violation (federal DOT standards apply)
Reckless driving causing injury
Gross negligence: leaving accident scene, falsifying logs/DVIRs, ignoring critical
safety warnings resulting in major damage or injury
Any repeated Level 4 offense

Drivers showing "improving" or "falling fast" trend phrases are excluded from the
Bottom Line but remain visible on page 4. Improvement pauses escalation; it does
not reset prior documented levels.
Coaching Backlog → Discipline Mapping (Samsara Page 3)
Drivers with ≥2 unacknowledged safety events appear on Coaching Needs Assigned.
Unacknowledged coaching after 14 days = Level 1 trigger (failure to complete
the coaching program). Ack is confirmed when all events in the 30-day Samsara
window show status: coached / dismissed / recognized.
Documentation Standard
Every disciplinary action requires:

Specific incident named (date, location, DOT inspection # if applicable)
49 CFR Part citation where a federal violation is involved
Clear statement of expected behavior change and timeline
Driver signature (or noted refusal)
Filed in Sharefile by Audra; working copies retained by Jeff and JB for Level 3+

Drafting: Jeff Hannahs drafts; Audra Newman files. Subject line = driver first name.

# Kozy Heat Fireplaces

Kozy Heat Fireplaces is a gas fireplace manufacturer headquartered in Lakefield, Minnesota. XFreight has been the carrier for their outbound lane to South Carolina for 3+ years. A dedicated spotted-trailer account with consistent long-haul volume.

## Summary

Long-haul dedicated account: Lakefield MN → Simpsonville SC (1,199 miles). Air-ride van service required (fireplace products). 2 trailers permanently spotted at Lakefield. Rate: ~$3,812–3,890/load. Estimated ~3–4 loads/month ≈ ~$11,500–15,500/month. X-TRUX INC asset hauls. Dispatched by Tajuan Jackson and Dan Heeren.

## Key Ideas

- **3+ year account** — long-standing relationship; among XFreight's most established dedicated-lane customers.
- **Air-ride van required** — fireplace products are fragile; only air-suspension trailers used. This requirement filters out many carriers and helps XFreight retain the lane.
- **2 dedicated trailers spotted** — trailers 2253211 and 2453247 are permanently assigned at Kozy Heat's Lakefield MN dock. One loads while the other is in transit.
- **Long-haul, high-rate** — 1,199 loaded miles one way to Simpsonville SC at ~$3,850/load is a high-value run (vs. regional hops at $400–700). Revenue per mile ~$3.21.
- **Single dedicated lane** — all loads are Lakefield MN → Simpsonville SC. No backhaul noted (drivers return empty or pick up a load from elsewhere in the Southeast).
- **Consistent volume** — visible in every daily upload reviewed (June 2026), with multiple in-transit loads per week.

## Lane Notes

The Lakefield MN → Simpsonville SC lane is ~1,200 miles one way. At HOS limits (~500–550 miles/day), transit is approximately 2–2.5 days one way. With 2 spotted trailers:
- Trailer A loads at Lakefield while Trailer B is in transit south.
- Trailer A dispatches south; Trailer B is spotted at delivery in SC (or driver returns to pick up a backhaul).
- Cycle time: ~7–10 days per load including driver recovery.
- Expected cadence: ~3–4 loads/month.

Backhaul opportunity: Simpsonville SC sits near Greenville/Spartanburg SC manufacturing corridor (BMW, Michelin, etc.). Return loads from that area are likely sourced through brokers.

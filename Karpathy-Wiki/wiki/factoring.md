# Factoring

XFreight evaluated four invoice-factoring vendors and **selected Triumph** (June 2026), with onboarding expected ~June 16–17, 2026. Factoring primarily addresses cash-flow gaps on slow-pay customers (e.g. AGCO's 60-day terms).

## Summary

Factoring converts unpaid invoices into immediate cash (minus a fee of ~1–1.5%). XFreight compared four vendors in late 2025: Pathward, Triumph, OTR Solutions, and eCapital. No vendor has been selected in the artifacts reviewed. The case for factoring strengthens if XFreight wins large, slow-pay customers in the next [[AGCO RFP]] cycle.

## Key Ideas

- Factoring fees: 1.0% (best offer) to 1.5% (eCapital standard); Quick Pay option for carriers at 2.5%.
- eCapital's offer targets the brokerage side (X-Linx) with carrier Quick Pay.
- eCapital requires a 12-month contract + 60-day termination notice.
- Decision not finalized in the artifacts reviewed.

## How Factoring Connects to the Pipeline

Per Alvys's own documentation:
1. Wire the factoring company into Alvys.
2. Invoices flow normally.
3. Batched + submitted to the factor.

The Alvys → QB reconciliation (pages 12–13 of the brief) and the AR aging tiles (page 1) are affected by factoring timing — factored invoices clear differently in the AR aging buckets.

## When Factoring Matters Most

- **Large slow-pay customers:** AGCO's 60-day terms would create a significant cash gap at scale. With factoring, XFreight gets paid in days; the factor waits the 60.
- **Brokerage quick pay (X-Linx):** eCapital's 2.5% quick-pay option pays carriers immediately. X-Linx would collect from the factor instead of waiting 30 days.
- **Fleet growth:** Adding trucks creates a cash-flow lag between spending on driver pay/fuel and collecting from customers.

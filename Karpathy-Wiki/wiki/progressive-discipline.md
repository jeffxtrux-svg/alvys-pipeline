# Progressive Discipline Policy

XFreight's formal 5-level framework for addressing driver performance and safety violations — codified June 16, 2026. Applies across X-Trux owner-operators and Truk-Way Leasing employee drivers.

## Summary

Two parallel 5-level tracks — one for **owner-operators** (contract action at Level 5), one for **Truk-Way employees** (employment termination at Level 5). Levels 1–3 are structurally identical across both tracks. The daily scorecard feeds directly into discipline entry points: Samsara page 4 speed flags map to levels; unacknowledged coaching backlog on page 3 triggers Level 1 after 14 days. CSA BASIC accumulation accelerates the escalation path. Severe incidents bypass Levels 1–2.

**Status:** Drafted June 16, 2026 from Jeff Hannahs / Claude session. Pending formal sign-off by JB Sweere. To be incorporated into the next revision of the X-Trux Driver Safety Manual (Audra Newman, last issued January 1, 2022).

## Key Ideas

- **Two tracks** — Owner-operators (contract suspension/termination) and Truk-Way employees (unpaid suspension then termination). Levels 1–3 identical.
- **Brief is the trigger feed** — Samsara page 4 speed flags map directly to discipline levels; page 3 coaching backlog triggers Level 1 at 14 days unacknowledged.
- **Documentation non-negotiable** — Every action names the incident, cites the 49 CFR Part, states expected change, requires driver signature, and is filed by Audra in Sharefile.
- **Insurance can override** — Great West Casualty (via Acrisure/Jami Hewitt) may require driver removal from covered equipment independent of the internal discipline level.
- **CSA impact accelerates** — Violations approaching BASIC intervention thresholds (65th/80th percentile) warrant jumping levels.

## Owner-Operator Track (X-Trux Contract Carriers)

| Level | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Verbal Counseling** | First minor incident: incomplete DVIR, minor speeding, low-severity safety event | Verbal counseling + mandatory retraining/coaching; documented in driver file |
| **2 — Written Warning** | Repeat minor or failure to correct Level 1 | Written warning letter + safety improvement program; filed in Sharefile |
| **3 — Strong Written Notice** | Moderate incident, repeat of Level 2, or first serious violation | Stronger written notice + extended coaching + load restrictions (no high-value, time-critical, or specialized loads) |
| **4 — Load Suspension** | Serious or repeated; failure to improve after Level 3 | Suspension from preferred/primary loads 7–30 days (management discretion based on severity) |
| **5 — Contract Action** | Severe or repeated; failure to improve after Level 4 | Contract suspension or termination |

**Severe-incident entry points:** Preventable accidents, major moving violations (reckless driving, excessive speed, failure to obey traffic control), scale violations, and moving citations affecting CSA BASIC scores **bypass Levels 1–2 and enter at Level 3 or higher.** Management sets Level 3 vs. Level 4 based on severity and history.

## Truk-Way Employee Track

| Level | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Verbal Counseling** | First minor incident | Verbal counseling + mandatory retraining/coaching; documented |
| **2 — Written Warning** | Repeat minor or failure to correct Level 1 | Written warning + safety improvement program; filed in Sharefile |
| **3 — Strong Written Notice** | Moderate incident, repeat of Level 2, or first serious violation | Stronger written notice + extended coaching + load/route restrictions |
| **4 — Unpaid Suspension + Final Warning** | Serious or repeated; failure to improve after Level 3 | Unpaid suspension 3–14 days + final written warning; return requires management sign-off |
| **5 — Termination** | Severe or repeated; failure to improve after Level 4 | Employment terminated |

Severe-incident entry points are the same as the OO track — enter at Level 3 or higher.

## Immediate Termination Grounds (Both Tracks)

No progressive steps. Applies to owner-operators (contract) and employees (employment):

- Fatal accident where the driver is found at fault
- DUI or controlled substance violation (federal DOT drug/alcohol rules apply)
- Reckless driving causing injury to any person
- Gross negligence: leaving the scene of an accident, falsifying logs/DVIRs or any regulatory record, ignoring critical safety warnings resulting in major vehicle damage or injury
- Any repeated Level 4 offense

## Speed Flag → Discipline Mapping (Samsara Page 4)

| Samsara Flag | Peak % | Discipline Entry |
|---|---|---|
| "Watch this driver" / "Where is the fire?" | 1.25–1.99% | Informal coaching; document in driver file |
| "Driver needs a conversation" / "This is too fast" | 2.0–2.49% | **Level 1** verbal counseling |
| "Need to sit down with this driver" | ≥ 2.5% | **Level 1**; Level 2 if no improvement in 30 days |
| "STOP this driver now" | ≥ 3.0% | **Level 2** minimum; Level 3 if prior speed history exists |
| Bottom Line — two consecutive weeks | — | **Automatic Level 2** regardless of trend |

Drivers showing "improving — keep it up" or "falling fast — keep it up" trend phrases are **excluded from the Bottom Line** escalation but remain visible on page 4. Improvement pauses escalation; it does not reset prior documented levels.

## Coaching Backlog → Discipline Mapping (Samsara Page 3)

Drivers with ≥ 2 unacknowledged safety events appear on the Coaching Needs Assigned list.

- **Unacknowledged coaching after 14 days = Level 1 trigger** (verbal counseling on failure to complete the coaching program).
- Ack is confirmed when all events in the 30-day Samsara window have status `coached / dismissed / recognized`. See [[Coaching Ack]] for how ack state is derived.
- Drivers stay on the list until all events are acked, then roll off after 3 more days.

## CSA Threshold Guidance

| BASIC Category | Intervention Threshold | Action at That Level |
|---|---|---|
| Unsafe Driving / Crash Indicator | 65th percentile | Accelerate discipline; flag to JB |
| Maintenance / HOS / Vehicle Maint / Driver Fitness | 80th percentile | Accelerate discipline; fleet-wide memo from Audra |

If Maintenance BASIC exceeds **60th percentile**, issue a fleet-wide pre-trip reinforcement memo proactively. Any driver generating an additional Maintenance citation at that point skips to Level 3 entry.

## Documentation Standards

Every disciplinary action — verbal or written — must be documented with:

1. **Specific incident** — date, location, DOT inspection number if applicable.
2. **49 CFR Part citation** — where a federal regulation is involved.
3. **Expected change** — what behavior must change and by when.
4. **Driver signature** — or a noted refusal to sign.
5. **Filing** — copy goes to driver's Sharefile incident folder; Jeff and JB retain working copies for Level 3+.

**Drafting:** Jeff Hannahs drafts warning letters; **Audra Newman files** and maintains the incident record. Subject line pattern: driver first name (e.g., "Brad") for quick inbox recognition.

## Recordkeeping Location

All incident records maintained in Sharefile:

```
Sharefile – Audra – safety
└── new driver truck printouts

Sharefile – incident file – 2014–current
├── By year
└── By driver

Accidents – last 3 years by driver
└── (subset of incident file)
```

**Note:** The pipeline does NOT parse Sharefile. Historic incident data is not surfaced in the daily brief. Samsara catches new safety events; DOT roadside inspections flow monthly through FMCSA/MCMIS → SambaSafety CSA Scorecard (brief page 10).

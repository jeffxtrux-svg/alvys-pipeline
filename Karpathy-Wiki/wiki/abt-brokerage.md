# ABT Brokerage

ABT Brokerage is a freight brokerage firm and one of XFreight's largest and longest-standing brokerage customers. A ~2-year relationship (as of 2026). ABT tenders loads to both X-TRUX INC (asset hauls) and X-LINX INC (where X-LINX then finds carriers). Two primary pick-up origins: Cassleton ND and Philadelphia PA.

## Summary

High-volume brokerage customer active across both X-TRUX and X-LINX. 15 open X-TRUX invoices visible in a 24-day AR snapshot, rates ranging $2,400–5,250/load. X-LINX also brokers ABT loads through third-party carriers ($2,700–5,500/load). Two primary origins: Cassleton ND (agricultural corridor) and Philadelphia PA (Northeast manufacturing/distribution). Estimated combined monthly revenue: ~$15,000–25,000+ depending on load mix.

## Key Ideas

- **~2-year relationship** — established brokerage customer with consistent load volume across multiple lanes.
- **Dual entity involvement** — X-TRUX hauls some ABT loads directly; X-LINX brokers other ABT loads to third-party carriers. ABT functions as both a shipper/tender source and a pass-through brokerage.
- **Two distinct origin corridors:**
  - **Cassleton ND** — eastern North Dakota, near Fargo. Likely agricultural or agri-processing freight (Red River Valley grain/sugar beet corridor). Multiple X-TRUX loads originate here.
  - **Philadelphia PA** — Northeast manufacturing or distribution. X-TRUX hauls these loads to Midwest/Plains destinations.
- **Wide rate range** — $2,400–5,250/load (X-TRUX asset hauls); $2,700–5,500/load (X-LINX brokered). Higher end likely reflects longer lanes or specialized loads.
- **High invoice count** — 15 X-TRUX + 4 X-LINX open invoices in a 24-day snapshot (June 2026). One of the most active brokerage customers in terms of load count.

## Relationship Structure

ABT Brokerage operates in two modes with XFreight:

1. **ABT → X-TRUX (asset hauls):** ABT tenders loads from their shipper clients to XFreight. X-TRUX drivers haul the load. XFreight invoices ABT; ABT pays XFreight. Most load volume flows this way.

2. **ABT → X-LINX (brokered):** X-LINX brokers some ABT freight to third-party carriers. X-LINX earns a margin on the spread. Carriers used include Guarantee Safety LLC, Nova Carriers Inc, BF Prime LLC.

## Origin Lane Notes

**Cassleton ND corridor:**
Cassleton is located in the Red River Valley (Cass County ND), one of the most productive agricultural areas in North America (wheat, corn, soybeans, sugar beets). ABT likely brokers freight from agri-processing facilities in that corridor (American Crystal Sugar, POET Ethanol, grain elevators, etc.). Loads from Cassleton to Midwest/Plains destinations are a natural fit for X-TRUX's lane structure.

**Philadelphia PA corridor:**
Loads originating in Philadelphia likely come from manufacturing or distribution hubs in the Delaware Valley. These are longer hauls (Philadelphia to SD/ND is ~1,600–1,900 miles) explaining the higher rates ($4,000–5,250/load) in that origin set.

# Johnson Brothers of South Dakota

Johnson Brothers of South Dakota is the South Dakota distribution arm of Johnson Brothers, a national wine and spirits distributor. XFreight has historical load history with Johnson Brothers and has been awarded new everyday freight on a Sioux Falls → Rapid City dedicated lane, using per-pallet pricing.

## Summary

Newly awarded dedicated daily lane: Sioux Falls SD → Rapid City SD (~350 miles). Pricing: **$60/pallet + FSC %**. Will become everyday business — a daily dedicated run. Currently routed through X-LINX (Button Enterprises LLC), but daily volume warrants eventual X-TRUX dedicated truck consideration. At ~17–19 pallets/load and daily cadence, estimated monthly revenue ~$20,000–28,000.

## Key Ideas

- **Newly awarded everyday business** — Sioux Falls → Rapid City is a new dedicated lane that will run daily. This is a strategic win: SD's two largest cities, consistent beverage freight.
- **Per-pallet pricing: $60/pallet + FSC%** — unlike flat-rate or mileage-based pricing, revenue scales with pallet count per load. This means revenue varies by delivery size but is predictable when pallet counts are consistent.
- **Historical relationship** — invoice #1007279 is a prior period load still in AR (29+ days), showing XFreight has run Johnson Brothers freight before the new lane was formalized.
- **Currently X-LINX / Button Enterprises** — loads are currently brokered via X-LINX with Button Enterprises LLC as the carrier (same carrier that runs the Billion Auto RPC lane). As volume ramps to daily, X-TRUX dedicated equipment should be evaluated.
- **Beverage distribution lane** — Johnson Brothers distributes wine, spirits, and beer. Freight is pallet-stacked cases — heavy, stable loads. No special equipment required (standard dry van). Alcohol distribution follows retailer delivery schedules.
- **SF → RC is ~350 miles** — a full-day round trip. One dedicated truck could handle this lane 5 days/week.

## Operational Notes

- **Lane: Sioux Falls → Rapid City** (~350 miles, I-90 corridor). Return is empty or backhaul opportunity (RC is a tourism/destination market — limited inbound freight vs. outbound consumer goods).
- **Carrier today: Button Enterprises LLC** (X-LINX brokered). Button also runs the Billion Auto RPC lane. As Johnson Brothers scales to daily, evaluate whether Button can absorb both commitments or whether a second truck/carrier is needed.
- **Backhaul gap**: SF→RC is a net-outbound lane for consumer goods. Finding consistent back-haul loads from RC to SF (or pickup at RC for further points east) would significantly improve lane economics for X-TRUX if dedicated.
- **Alcohol freight note**: No special licensing needed for interstate transport of packaged alcohol (common carrier). Delivery windows and retailer sign-off requirements may apply.

# Dan Tracking + Driver Connection

Dan Heeren (Logistics Manager) shares Jeff's facts-first tracking philosophy but adds a sharp skeptical edge: he does not trust a number until he can prove it wrong. He is also the leadership team's most driver-connected member, with product implications for any brief or report aimed at drivers.

## Summary

Dan is closest to Jeff's mental model — only counts delivered, real data — but differs in that he actively tries to disprove any number he reads. Every metric on a brief or report Dan receives must be traceable to a single underlying record. He also sponsors the planned per-driver report ([[Driver Report Wishlist]]) because of his direct driver relationships.

## Tracking Style

- **Facts-first:** wants only numbers that are already real (delivered, billed, invoiced). Not forecasts.
- **Skeptical:** reads any reported figure with intent to **prove it wrong**. He does not trust a number until it survives pressure.
- **Practical implication for any brief sent to Dan:** every metric must be **defensible at the source** — traceable to a single cell / load / record, not a model output or a smoothed average without provenance.
- **Product principle:** include underlying counts and links to raw rows on Dan's pages. The MPG / speed / idle pages on the Operational brief section already follow this pattern; keep it.

## Driver Connection

- Dan is **the most driver-connected leader** on the team. He talks to drivers frequently, knows their personal situations, and tracks individual context the rest of the leadership team does not have.
- He wants **as much information as possible** about each driver: operational, safety, financial, location, status.
- The planned **Operations brief** (per [[Brief Roadmap]]) should be the most data-dense of the briefs — Dan reads deeply, not skims. Do not optimize his pages for brevity at the cost of detail.

## Implications for the Driver Report

Dan is the natural **product owner / sponsor** for the planned [[Driver Report Wishlist]]:
- He owns the driver relationship question — drivers will ask him "is this number right?"
- He will be the natural QA reviewer for what goes on the report.
- His skeptical read means the driver report must be as defensible as any brief page sent to him.

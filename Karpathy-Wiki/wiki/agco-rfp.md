# AGCO RFP

AGCO Corporation (NYSE: AGCO) is a global ag-equipment manufacturer ($50M+ annual TL spend). XFreight submitted a bid in the 2026 RFP cycle but **was not awarded any lanes** — outcome confirmed by email January 20, 2026.

## Summary

XFreight jointly bid (X-Trux asset + X-Linx brokerage) on AGCO's 2026 Truckload RFP. Despite "several lanes showing positive signs initially," the final awards published in January 2026 did not include XFreight. The relationship is intact for future cycles; next RFP is expected late 2026/early 2027.

## Key Ideas

- **AGCO outcome: NOT AWARDED.** No revenue impact in current period.
- AGCO North America spends $50M+ annually on truckload freight — even a partial award would be material.
- The bid structure is documented in detail below; useful for the next cycle.
- 60-day payment terms were a strategic concern — would have required factoring. See [[Factoring]].

## Outcome

**XFreight was not awarded any lanes.** Direct quote from Jeff Hannahs to AGCO's Erik Arana (Jan 20, 2026):

> *"We had several lanes that were showing positive signs initially, but as we know, the awards have been published yet... we were not awarded any lanes. Hopefully, we will have another opportunity in the future."*

**Why XFreight likely lost:** AGCO's criteria prioritize "best price per lane" and "low-cost carrier." Asset-based carriers (X-Trux) carry higher rates than asset-light brokers and large fleets. No reefer capacity for AGCO's Hazmat/Temp Control Van lanes was also a gap.

**Door is open:** Erik Arana (Manager, Logistics NA) responded politely; both sides expressed future interest. Next bid cycle likely **late 2026/early 2027** for 2027 rates.

### Award Logic

- AGCO does NOT award all business to one carrier — awards by best price per lane.
- Three matrices: High-Volume TL lanes (billing priority), Point-to-State pairs, State-to-Point pairs.
- Primary carrier designation per lane — enables capacity planning.

### Equipment Accepted

- 53' dry van (standard + Hazmat/Temp Control reefer for some lanes — gap for XFreight)
- Flatbed, Step deck, RGN, FBTSK/CT, OB variants
- Multi-stop loads (Pick→Pick→Pick→Drop combinations)

### Payment + Ops

- **60-day payment terms** — slow. Factoring would be relevant if volume were large.
- AGCO iTMS — carriers must accept tenders and update status through AGCO's TMS.
- No transloading; no co-loading.
- Standard AGCO FSC table + accessorial table.

### Insurance Requirements

Ranges from $250K to $750K cargo depending on location/trailer type. Standard $1M auto + GL + workers' comp.

## Lessons for Next Cycle

1. **Pricing is the gap** — AGCO's primary criterion is lowest price per lane. Asset-based carrier rates are structurally higher.
2. **Reefer gap** — cannot bid Hazmat/Temp Control Van lanes without adding reefer capacity (via X-Linx brokerage to reefer carriers).
3. **60-day terms** — build factoring capacity before bidding AGCO-scale volumes. See [[Factoring]].
4. **4flow relationship** — operations go through 4flow; building that relationship ahead of the next bid helps.
